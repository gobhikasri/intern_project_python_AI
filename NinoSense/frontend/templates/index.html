Understanding the ENSO Climate Cycle

A coupled ocean-atmosphere phenomenon impacting global weather variations

El Niño Phase

Characterized by unusual warming of surface waters in the eastern tropical Pacific Ocean. An ONI value of +0.5°C or higher denotes this phase, leading to heavy rainfall in Peru and droughts in East Asia/Australia.

Neutral Baseline

Corresponds to normal climatic ranges where the sea surface temperatures hover between -0.5°C and +0.5°C. Walker atmospheric cells operate under standard pressure guidelines.

La Niña Phase

Corresponds to the cooling phase where sea surface temperature anomalies drop to -0.5°C or below. This strengthens Walker circulation cells, bringing wetter winters to the North US and monsoons in India/Australia.
